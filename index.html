Nora Brooke Roggausch




Playfull, serious, consequent or impulsive, poetic or fierce - contradictory qualities surface in my painting process and are embodied in the results, which can be intimate little formats aswell as spatially expansive installations.
Neoncolors and symbolic abstract shapes are recurring elements in my formal vocabulary.

The work develops in a spontaneous, process based flow. Acrylic paint varying from translucent to opaque is applied in a variety of gestural strokes and delicate markmaking. Layers of open surfaces and specifically articulated shapes overlap until they interlock to a cohesive structure. I like to think of this abstract space as a personal universe and of the brookestyled shapes as celestial bodies following their own gravity - creating a sence of movement and speed or floating through space.

I strive for simplicity and the next moment for ostentatious flashyness. I care about joy, insecurity, sexyness. Performing such radical individuality is equally empowering, comforting and confrontative to me.
Throughout my artistic and educational practice I realized that the reflective intelligence used in making and experiencing artwork — in short: their meaning — is deeply connected with concepts of identity.

Art Education



I work with children, teenagers and adults, giving workshops in painting, printmaking, and spraying. I give tours and practice art education in exhibitionary environments like art fairs and art festivals. I am active at the artist collective Kunst Werk Stadt Berlin. In partnership with Berlin.Mondiale and the evangelical parish of Gropiusstadt we run a printing workshop for kids in Gropiusstadt Berlin. In my workshops I emphasize art as a tool to practice a differentiated reception and as a reason around which we can all gather together. I encourage workshop-participants to develop individual expressions rather then fulfill a certain canon. The goal is to experience self-efficacy.
I believe that the meaning of exhibitionary situations unfolds based on the background and identity of the artist and the mediator aswell as the viewer.
